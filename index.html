The studio

Every piece begins as narrow strips of cloth, woven on West African looms and worn at ceremonies. We give that cloth a second life as modern, made-to-order pieces — carrying a centuries-old craft to a wider world.

The artist

The hand behind the studio

British-born Nigerian · based in Nevis, St Kitts & Nevis

Siba has spent years developing her own language for West African strip cloth — translating its versatility and majesty into apparel, décor and tapestry that sit just as easily in a modern home as at a ceremony. Looms of Africa is the studio she created to share that work with the world.

“African strip-weave is just too beautiful to be forgotten or discarded.”— Usibakaiye
The craft

Woven by hand,
one strip at a time

Strip-weave is among Africa's oldest textile traditions. On narrow looms, weavers like Mr Kola in Iseyin, Nigeria handcraft long bands of Aso-oke — hours of work each — then join them edge to edge into whole cloth.

Much of the cloth we work with is recovered from ceremonies: pieces no longer worn, too beautiful to throw away. Siba reimagines them into wearable art and pieces for the home.

The work

Cognition

A body of work in three forms — sculptural busts, dressed figures, and tapestries woven to shift with the light.

Charlestown Gallery — Nevis
November – December 2023

Olori

Ten queen heads

Ten fabric busts in Yoruba Aso-oke, worn in full gele head-form — a tribute to the modern Yoruba woman, and a soft-textile answer to the carved wood and bronze heads of West African tradition.

Mannequin Mann

Dressed figures

Strip cloth reassembled as contemporary apparel and statement coats, styled on the figure.

Tapestries

Art that changes with the light

Wall hangings woven from Aso-oke silk, set so the metallic threads catch and shift as the light moves across them — from the Giraffe, Horse, Elephant and Africa Man series.
